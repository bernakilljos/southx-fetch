1.

Declaration of Pecuniary Interests

  • View the background to item 1.

Under Standing Order 4.4, where any councillor has a Disclosable Pecuniary Interest (as defined in the Members’ Code of Conduct (para. 4)) in any matter to be considered at a meeting of the Council, a committee, sub-committee or joint committee, they must withdraw from the meeting room during the whole of the consideration of that matter and must not participate in any vote on that matter unless a dispensation has been obtained from the Monitoring Officer.

 

Minutes:

There were no declarations of pecuniary interests.

2.

Declarations of Other Interests

A Councillor may have a non-pecuniary interest that they would wish to disclose.

 

Examples of this may include:

 

·         A member declaring that they have received correspondence from an applicant/objector in the past. This can be about the application, but the Member confirms that they have an open mind during consideration of the item.

·         A Member declaring that they have attended a public meeting on the application but have an open mind during consideration of the item.

·         A Member is acquainted with the applicant but that this association would not influence their decision.

 

Members declaring any other interests are not required to withdraw from the debate and these declarations are made only to ensure transparency during the decision making process.

Minutes:

Case No. 24/01346/FUL (agenda Item 6, page 117 of the agenda pack, page 13 of the first addendum and page 1 of the second addendum).

 

Officers introduced the item.  It was noted there were further changes to the report. These changes included noting additional representations that were received, which included a Fire Risk Appraisal, and a Daylight, Sunlight and Overshadowing Report. Officers made further changes to the report at paragraph 3.2.102 in respect of 2 windows (40a and 40b) failing the VSC assessment under the BRE guide; and at paragraph 3.2.117 in respect of the overshadowing assessment, noting there is not a communal amenity space at fifth floor, but the impacts are still considered acceptable. 

 

Three interested parties spoke in opposition to the application.

 

One interested party spoke in support of the application.

 

Councillor David Oxley, Ward Councillor for Stockwell West and Larkhall addressed the Committee to speak against the application.

 

Officers responded to questions from Members.

 

With the Chair’s agreement, a Technical Specialist addressed the Committee on the applicant’s behalf.

 

Further to a discussion, the Chair moved to vote on the Officer’s recommendation to approve the application.

 

The Chair wanted it minuted that the committee noted that the nearest healthy route to the site is Stockwell Gardens and that this should be reflected in the section 106 deed.

 

Subject to:

·       An informative to encourage the developer to liaise with the neighbouring site at Singer Mews in respect of their fire safety remedial works.

·       An informative to note that Ward Councillors should be consulted in respect of use of the financial contribution secured in this application for Play Space improvements.
