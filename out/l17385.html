Minutes:

The Chair introduced the agenda item and noted that the Committee had looked at the first set of savings proposals at its meeting in November 2025 and the Council remained in a very challenging financial position. The Committee was asked to review an updated set of savings proposals at this meeting and consider whether the Council would be able to close the funding gap and would the results still allow for Lambeth’s services to deliver to the standard expected by residents.

The Acting Cabinet Member for Finance, Councillor Judith Cavanagh introduced the report and noted that:

  • The report contained an additional set of savings that were necessary in light of the financial pressures on the Council’s budget. It was noted that those financial pressures continued to be experienced in the Council’s larger service areas such as provisions for those in temporary accommodation, Adult Social Care and Children's Services.
  • While the Council was facing those financial pressures, it was still performing well and was delivering a good quality of service. This was demonstrated by the ‘Good’ rating that was achieved recently by the borough’s Youth Justice Service.
  • The Government’s commitment to supporting local authorities to deliver for their residents was demonstrated by providing them with multi-year financial settlements to help them plan more effectively for the future. The Council was pleased that it had received a larger financial settlement from the Government than had been predicted, but it was still not sufficient to bridge the funding gap, therefore, they would be lobbying the Government for further additional financial support. The Council had been granted a transition arrangement of three years to help it improve its financial position, but this may be inhibited by the fact it was one of four local authorities that had been ‘capped’ by the Government, therefore the four local authorities were in discussions with Ministers regarding this matter.
  • It was refreshing that there was finally a Government in place that was committed to improve the lives for the country’s citizens and their commitment to lift the two-child limit which restricted the additional support for children on Universal Credit (UC) was an example of this. The Council estimated approximately 4,000 children would benefit from this policy to help to lift children out of poverty.
  • In conclusion, the Acting Cabinet Member for Finance was confident they would be able to close the funding gap with measures outlined which also allowed them to propose a balanced budget for 2026-27 leading to a much more secure financial position in 2027-28.

Developer Contributions PDF 2 MB

Contact for enquiries: Matthew Dibben, Director of Regeneration, Growth & Capital Investment, mdibben@lambeth.gov.uk.

Minutes:

The Chair introduced the item and stated that developer contributions helped the Council to  deliver the infrastructure and services communities needed to support the developments being built in their communities. This meeting would give the Committee an opportunity to assess how well the Council was performing in terms of distributing those developer contributions.

 

Deputy Leader (Housing, Investment and New Homes), Councillor Danny Adilypour, introduced the report and noted that:

 

  • The report provided a comprehensive overview of the Council’s approach to distributing developer contributions and it also provided some examples of how the Council was ensuring that the money was spent to benefit communities across the borough.
  • He noted that there were three main ways in how the Council obtained developer contributions - Community Infrastructure Levy (CIL), Neighbourhood CIL (NCIL) and Section 106 planning obligations (S106). The Committee heard how the report outlined what the Council was doing to improve and highlight transparency, improve delivery of the projects and what it was also doing to ensure that the distribution of developer contributions aligned with the Council’s Growth Plan, Lambeth Local Plan, Infrastructure Delivery Plan and Borough Plan.
  • The report also provided an overview of development contributions received by the Council as of December 2025, along with projections of what this is likely to look like in the years ahead, although with the caveat that this was subject to change due to broader economic conditions, variations in development activity, and potential changes in national policy or legislation.
  • Officers provided additional information as requested by Committee Members and he welcomed any comments or recommendations which helped the Council to build on the work it was already delivering in this policy area.

 

The Chair invited witnesses to speak on the agenda item.

Trash Gxng focused on supporting those who are marginalised in the community and often those who may be economically disadvantaged or facing multiple barriers, with Special Educational and Disabilities (SEND) and mental health needs as examples.

He also noted that Trash Gxng were also able to launch programmes through the support of this funding and the aid of the Council’s  ‘Lambeth Made’ programme.
