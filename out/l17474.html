Declaration of Pecuniary Interests

Under Standing Order 4.4, where any councillor has a Disclosable Pecuniary Interest (as defined in the Members’ Code of Conduct (para. 4)) in any matter to be considered at a meeting of the Council, a committee, sub-committee or joint committee, they must withdraw from the meeting room during the whole of the consideration of that matter and must not participate in any vote on that matter unless a dispensation has been obtained from the Monitoring Officer.

Update on the Council's Action Plan response to the External Auditor Recommendations under paragraph 2 of Schedule 7 of the Local Audit and Accountability Act 2014 PDF 261 KB

Contact for enquiries: Zena Cooke, Corporate Director of Resources, zcooke@lambeth.gov.uk, 0207926 6943

 

Additional documents:

  • Appendix A - Action Plan , item 4. PDF 744 KB

Minutes:

The Corporate Director of Resources presented the report, it was noted that this was a regular report and additional actions had been included compared with previous reports. The report set out the latest position. Members were advised that as this Committee meeting was taking place prior to the two February Cabinet meetings a fuller version would be presented to the Committee in March 2026. A budget report would be considered by Cabinet on 02 February 2025, which would provide the Council’s final savings proposals, along with 2025-2026 Financial Performance Report for Quarter three. The full budget for 2026-27 and the treasury management strategy, the capital strategy and programme, the Housing Revenue Account and Dedicated Schools grant was due at Cabinet on 23 February 2026.

 

In response to Members’ questions, it was explained that:

 

  • Identifying further savings over and above those already identified in demand led services was challenging;
  • Discretionary spending was under continuous review and could be reprofiled as required;
  • The threshold for spend control panel approval would be reduced from £10,000 to £5,000;
  • Full Council would receive a budget report that would set out the capital strategy and strategic asset management framework. This would explain how the council manage assets and would include the asset disposal policy;
  • The Council was developing a Housing Revenue Account (HRA) financial recovery plan, details of which would be added to the action plan;
  • The property market remained challenging; however, Cabinet had approved the commencement of the HRA disposals;
  • Two tranches of HRA disposals had already been completed, with properties achieving or exceeding market value;
  • The most recent HRA disposal tranche was estimated to generate £10-£11m income;
  • Forvis-Mazars had completed extensive work on leaseholder charge objections, but further discussions with the Council were required to determine their impact;
  • Statutory recommendation four was related to Homes for Lambeth (HfL), not the HRA.
  • Other recommendations highlighting financial resilience and reserves applied to both the HRA and General Fund;
  • Recommendation two had a significant target; directorates had developed business cases and undergone challenge sessions to provide detailed savings information;
  • Directorates had been asked to complete savings delivery plans, implications, and timelines for achieving their savings;
  • Projects not yet in contract, not fully externally funded or were not related to safety critical compliance spend had been paused whilst a review of the capital programme was completed;
  • Cabinet Members regularly reviewed the paused capital programme, and projects affecting savings delivery were only paused where borrowing costs outweighed capital savings;
  • Paused projects would now be reviewed to identify invest-to-save opportunities without additional borrowing;
  • HfL activities had been brought in-house, and its accounts were reviewed, with financial outcomes incorporated into the Council’s accounts;
  • A review of HfL, was being commissioned to assess options for the future of HfL with the objective of maximising recovery of the HfL loan;
  • Housing officers were identifying how the existing HfL company structure could reduce the cost of temporary accommodation for the Council; model used successfully in neighbouring boroughs.

 

RESOLVED: Corporate Committee  ...  view the full minutes text for item 4.

Minutes:

The Risk Manager introduced the report. It was noted that the Risk Management Strategy and Policy were in their final year and would be fully updated for 2027-2030. No major changes had been made as part of the annual review, however, there has been some updates, which are set out in paragraph 1.5 of the cover report.

  • Appendix A - updated key risk summary Q3 25-26 , item 7. PDF 261 KB

  2. Considered and unanimously agreed that each directorate presents its most significant risks for oversight and assurance to the Committee on a cyclical basis to ensure the Committee received assurance on a wider range of directorate level risks.

9.

Counter Fraud Progress Report 2025-26 PDF 377 KB

Contact for enquiries: Michael O’Reilly, Head of Counter Fraud, 07792 159928, moreilly2@lambeth.gov.uk.

Additional documents:

  • Appendix 1 - Tables summarising investigation activity , item 9. PDF 521 KB

Minutes:

The Head of Counter Fraud introduced the report, it was noted that this was a standard progress update report. The Council had secured a further 19 properties this month, which was ahead of target. Training for contract managers of fraud awareness continued, and the peer review details requested at a previous Committee meeting had been referenced in the report.

 

In response to Members’ questions, it was explained that:

 

  • The timeline for recovering a property varied depending on the complexity and circumstances of each case;
  • If a tenant agreed to surrender the property, recovery could be achieved within weeks;
  • Where a tenant did not agree to leave, the Council would have to pursue civil recovery through the courts, which could take up to 12 months;
  • Lambeth worked with the National Fraud Initiative (NFI) and the London Fraud Hub, which involved 26 London boroughs sharing agency and payroll data to identify potential cases of individuals holding secondary jobs in another borough;
  • NFI was still being rolled out nationally, and all Council’s would be required to join;

 

Members congratulated the team on the successful peer review and asked future reports include the different types of internal fraud cases. The Chair referred to recent news articles on “polygamous working” and she felt reassured that the Council were part of the NFI in order to identify this, she felt that a driver for this was the working from home culture that came about during COVID.

 

RESOLVED: Corporate Committee considered the progress and outcomes of the counter fraud activity for 2025/26 and the Committee unanimously agreed to receive the updated Counter Fraud Strategy at the March 2026 meeting.

Pay Policy Statement 2026-2027 PDF 310 KB

Contact for enquiries: Sherene Russell-Alexander, Director of HR and OD, 020 7926 7419, SRussellAlexander@lambeth.gov.uk

 

Additional documents:

  • Appendix A - Lambeth Pay Policy Statement - 2026-27 , item 10. PDF 328 KB
  • Appendix B - Pay Policy Statement (As of November 2025) , item 10. PDF 432 KB
  • Appendix C - Lambeth Payscales_2025-2026 , item 10. PDF 284 KB

Minutes:

The report was introduced by the Director of HR and Organisational Development (OD) who presented the Committee with a statement on the Council’s Pay Policy. She confirmed that the statement fulfilled statutory requirements and highlighted that the Chief Executive’s pay was subject to the national frameworks, and that senior officers did not receive bonuses or performance-based pay. The statement set out the relationship between the lowest and highest paid employees in the Council, with the Chief Executive making 4.8X the median salary, and 7.6X the lowest paid employee’s salary. The Director of HR and OD clarified that no average multiple exists regarding pay generally, but that Lambeth is in good keeping with its nearest neighbours. It was also clarified that the statement must be approved by Full Council and that its changes would take full effect in 2026/7 following this.

 

In response to Member’s questions, it was explained that:

 

  • The decrease in the pay ratio was not indicative of a greater number of lower-paid employees.
  • The current Chief Executive’s pay is greater than that of his predecessor.
  • The gender pay differential had decreased compared to last year.
  • The Inner London Weighting Allowance was incorporated into Council salaries.
  • The lowest-paid figure earned more than they did last year, but the median figure had relatively stayed the same.

 

The Chair thanked the Director of HR and OD for presenting the Statement and concluded the report.

 

RESOLVED: The Committee unanimously agreed to recommend the Pay Policy Statement to Full Council for approval on 04 March 2026.

Calculation of Council Tax Base and National Non-Domestic Rates Return (NNDR1) 2026/27 PDF 278 KB

Amaris Wong, Group Manager, Strategic Finance, awong1@lambeth.gov.uk

 

Additional documents:

  • Appendix 1 - The Calculation of the Council Tax Base 26-27 , item 11. PDF 122 KB

Minutes:

The report was introduced by the Director of Resources, who shared that it comes annually to the Committee, and that the Council was required to set the Council Tax base before 31 March 2026. It was explained that the Council Tax base was determined by the number of dwellings adjusted to the Council Tax band which the Council was able to charge Council Tax for. This in turn helped form a calculation of Council Tax income which would form part of the budget report that would go to Full Council. The Committee were advised that the NNDR1 forms would become available after the date of the meeting and the Corporate Director of Resources would need to finalise and approve the NNDR1 form setting out the business rate tax base.

 

In response to Member’s questions, it was explained that:

 

  • The decrease in the Council Tax Base from 2025/6 to 2026/7 relied on a variety of factors, such as the number of empty properties in the borough, and that the accuracy of the estimates relied on many variables.
  • The Council Tax Base and its income calculation had been factored into the Medium-Term Financial Strategy (MTFS).
  • That there were two percentage statistics regarding Council Tax collection rates in the report: actual and eventual, with the eventual rate being 96.5%.
  • The collection rate was higher than it had been previously.
  • Reasons for the higher collection rate included several variables, such as payment plans, as well as a greater focus by the finance team to increase collection rates.

 

RESOLVED: That the Committee unanimously:

  1. Approved the calculation and the amount calculated as the Council Tax base for the year 2026/27 at 116,594 (118,324 in 2025/26) as set out in appendix 1(b).
  2. Approved the schedule of discounts and premiums in paragraph 2.14.
  3. Note the proposed changes to the Council Tax Support Scheme (CTS) have been assumed for planning purposes in setting the tax base. Should a decision be taken not to proceed with the proposed changes to CTS scheme this would be addressed in the calculation of the taxbase for 2027/28 and the shortfall would be reflected in updated Medium Term Financial Strategy (MTFS) calculations.
  4. Delegated authority to the Corporate Director of Resources and S151 Officer to finalise and approve the NNDR1 form setting out the business rate tax base.

Procurement and Contract Management update PDF 326 KB

 

Contact for enquiries: Mark Pearson, Assistant Director, Procurement and Contract Management, 07847 883097, mpearson@lambeth.gov.uk

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

                                                     

Additional documents:

  • Appendix 1 - Procurement and Contract Management actions , item 12. PDF 226 KB

Minutes:

The report was introduced by the Assistant Director of Procurement and Contract management, who stated that the report summarised concerns from the Committee meeting held on 25 November 2026 regarding purchasing compliance, contract management, and social values. Within the report, an action plan was presented alongside actions management have taken to address those concerns and drive improvements in compliance.

 

In response to Member’s questions, it was explained that:

 

  • Breakdowns were made by service area regarding non-compliance with purchase order procedures, and these were provided on a quarterly basis and shared with Directorates.
  • Improvements in the level of compliance across different service areas have been made. The Corporate Director for Resources confirmed that a fuller update could be brought to the Committee meeting in March 2026.
  • Contract management is decentralised across the Council, and compliance accountability was handled by Directorates.
  • There was understanding among the Directorates that formal actions would be taken where there was consistent noncompliance, and there have been improvements in the process with the message that compliance should be 100%.
  • That management was taking non-compliance within purchase order procedures seriously.
  • Spend control is entwined with the procurement process and that the threshold has been lowered from £10K to £5K, with issues like retrospective purchase orders being more easily identified.
  • The culture around compliance with procurement procedure rules needs to be changed to improve compliance rates.

 

It was noted that the Committee wanted to see continued updates on this and could agree on full reassurance when this comes back, with the Chair stating they did not feel assured by the report, nor by current purchase order non-compliance. Members shared concerns that it was too early to tell that compliance efforts were a success and that while work was being done, the proof would be in the results which required a change in work culture as well as the efforts of the Procurement team.

 

RESOLVED: Corporate Committee agreed to consider assurance from the actions being taken to significantly improve performance as set out in paragraphs 2.16 to 2.23 2 and agreed to receive progress updates on the actions as part of future procurement update reports.
