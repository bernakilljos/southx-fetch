Minutes:

Presentation by the Licensing Officer

 

The Sub-Committee was informed that this was an application for a variation to the current premises licence. The applicant wished to add late night refreshments to their license and extend the venue hours of operation to 24 hours.

 

Monday – Sunday

23:00pm to 05:00am

 

Monday – Sunday

00:00am to 00:00am

 

Condition 8

There shall be no sales of single cans or bottles of beers, lagers, stout.

And ciders from premises.

 

Condition 20

Outside the sales of alcohol, all self-service areas of the alcohol shall be screened off to

Prevent access by customers and alcohol closed signs displayed clearly.

 

The Premises currently held a 24-hour license. The condition they wished to remove related to the sale of cans of beer and ales. Any cans of beer, alcoholic beverages, cider or similar products were required to be sold in a minimum quantity of four. The sale of single cans or bottles of beer, lager, stout or cider from the premises was not permitted.

In addition, outside the hours authorised for the sale of alcohol, and whilst the premises were open to the public, the licence holder was required to ensure that all alcohol within the premises was secured behind locked grills, locked screens or locked cabinet doors in order to prevent customer access. This condition was proposed to be replaced with wording referring to outside the hours for sale of alcohol. All self-service areas of the alcohol shall be screened off to prevent access to by customers and alcohol closed signs displayed clearly.

 

In attendance for the meeting was Mr Sharis Kanathpathy, the agent, and Mr. Faruk Arjun. Only one representation was submitted by the Licencing Authority relating to the requested timings within the application. The application form from the applicant could be found on pages 21 to 27 of the report. Representations against the application could be found on pages 29 to 31 of the report. The statement from the applicant could be found on pages 33 to 38 of the report and the location map and photos of the premises were on page 39 of the report.

 

The Licensing Officer informed the Sub-Committee that:

 

  • The Licensing Officer noted that they did not have precise data on how many 24-hour stores were in the borough however, they would be able to provide this information to Committee Members outside of the meeting for more clarity.
  • The Licensing Officer confirmed that the applicant was aware of the meeting that evening and that he would have been required to attend via Microsoft Teams in order to make his representation. The Licensing Officer confirmed that contact with the applicant would have taken place outside the meeting to notify him of the status of the application.
  • The Licensing Officer confirmed that the applicant has had many Temporary Event Notices (TEN`s) in the past to the early hours in the morning, and there has been no reports of anti-social behaviour from customers or staff on the premises.

Minutes:

Presentation by the Licensing Officer

 

The Sub-Committee was informed that this was an application for a variation to the current premises licence submitted by Stars Square Limited for the premises La Vuelta, 45 Tulse Hill, London, SW2 2TJ. The applicant sought permission to vary the hours for the sale of alcohol and to add late night refreshment for the following hours.

 

Sunday to Thursday

12:00pm to 22:30pm

 

Friday and Saturday

12:00pm to 00:30am

 

Friday and Saturday

23:00pm to 00:00am

 

Sunday to Thursday

07:30am to 23:00pm

 

Friday and Saturday

07:30am to 01:00am

 

Ten representations were received in relation to the application. These were submitted by the Licensing Authority, Regulatory Services and the Commercial and Technical Environmental Health Team (TEHT), together with eight local residents. The representations related to the prevention of public nuisance, the prevention of crime and disorder, and public safety. Copies of the representations were provided to the applicant, and the applicant had been invited to attend the hearing.

Licensing Officers had attempted to contact the applicant by email on two occasions, namely on 15 January 2026 and 23 January 2026. In attendance on behalf of the responsible authorities was Ms Marcia Green, together with Mr Matthew Onakoya representing the Commercial and Technical Environmental Health Team.

Two local residents were also in attendance, Miss Camilla Walker, whose representation appeared on pages 57 to 61 of the report, and Mr Peter Learnat, whose representation appeared on pages 71 to 73 of the report. The application form was contained on pages 45 to 52 of the report. Representations in opposition to the application were included on pages 53 to 80 of the report, and a copy of the existing licence was contained on pages 81 to 93 of the report.

 

Presentation by Interested Parties

 

Marcia Green, representing the Licensing Authority, said that:

 

  • The Licensing Authority submitted a representation due to concerns that the variation, as applied for, may undermined two licensing objectives, namely the prevention of public nuisance and the prevention of crime and disorder. The applicant sought to extend the hours for the sale of alcohol, add late night refreshment on Fridays and Saturdays, and extend the overall opening hours. These hours were significantly later than those permitted under the existing licence.
  • Under the Council’s current licensing policy, the premises was classed as a restaurant located within a local shopping parade. For this type of premises in this location, the recommended terminal hours were 23:00 hours from Sunday to Thursday and midnight on Fridays and Saturdays. The hours requested by the applicant exceeded these recommendations.
  • The premises was situated within a residential area, with homes located directly above, opposite, and within the surrounding streets. This factor was considered significant when assessing the potential for late-night disturbance. Extending alcohol sales until 12:30 am and opening hours until 1:00 am increased the likelihood of late-night noise and disturbance affecting local residents, particularly those residing above the premises.

Presentation by the Licensing Officer

 

The Sub-Committee was informed that this was an application for a review of the current premises licence brought forward by the Metropolitan Police, following a number of incidents that had occurred at the premises. In attendance for the Metropolitan Police was PC David Watson and on behalf of the applicant was Mr David Dadd’s. One representation was submitted by the Licensing Authority supporting the polices review of the premises.

 

The application form from the police could be found on pages 104 to 105 of the report. The Licensing Authority representation could be found on pages 107 to 108 of the report. The petition support of the premises was on pages 109 to 110 of the report, and a copy of the premises licence was on pages 111 to 121.

 

Presentation by the Applicant

 

Mr. David Dadd`s, Legal representative for Aquarium Tapas Bar, informed the Sub-Committee that:

 

  • Mr Dadd firstly thanked the Metropolitan Police Service and colleagues for meeting with him on several occasions during the review period. He also expressed his thanks to Democratic Services and the Licensing Officers for their work throughout this time.
  • He stated that the review had centred on a single isolated incident in March 2025, which had not repeated itself. He acknowledged that the licensing objectives had been undermined; however, those matters had been addressed. He confirmed that they had been addressed through conditions, with the Police acting as the principal source of advice on crime and disorder, and that the Police had been satisfied that the proposed steps would promote the licensing objectives.
  • Mr Dadd`s noted that the licence had originally contained 15 conditions. He stated that the revised conditions submitted to the Committee and the Licensing Authority would, in effect, supersede the existing conditions in order to tidy up the licence. He confirmed that, following discussions between himself, the Police, and the Licensing Authority, condition number 8 had been removed, resulting in 14 conditions.
  • He further stated that it had been agreed that these conditions would replace the previous conditions in their entirety and that the exercise had been intended to rationalise the licence.

 

In response to questions from Members, the Mr Dadd`s confirmed:

 

  • Mr Dadd stated that, during several meetings with the Police and the licensee, it had been established that the Police lacked confidence in the existing DPS. He stated that both he and the Police had held concerns regarding the DPS and whether he had exercised sufficient control over patrons.
  • However, he confirmed that the licensee had since addressed those concerns through the appointment of a new DPS. He further stated that the proposed DPS held a personal licence, which had addressed all concerns going forward.
